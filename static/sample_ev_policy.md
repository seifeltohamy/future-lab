# Global Electric Vehicle Policy Report 2024

## Executive Summary

The global transition to electric vehicles (EVs) is accelerating, driven by government mandates, falling battery costs, and growing consumer awareness of climate change. This report summarizes key policies, major players, and market dynamics as of Q4 2024.

## Key Entities

### Governments & Regulators

- **European Union**: Passed legislation banning the sale of new internal combustion engine (ICE) vehicles by 2035. The EU has allocated €3.5 billion for public charging infrastructure.
- **United States (EPA)**: Issued new emissions standards requiring 56% of new vehicle sales to be electric by 2032. The Inflation Reduction Act (IRA) provides $7,500 tax credits for qualifying EV purchases.
- **China (MIIT)**: World's largest EV market. Mandates that 40% of all auto sales be new energy vehicles (NEVs) by 2030. Subsidizes domestic manufacturers through a 15% corporate tax reduction.

### Major Manufacturers

- **Tesla**: Market leader in the US and Europe. CEO Elon Musk announced a $25,000 mass-market vehicle for 2025. Tesla holds 18% of global EV market share.
- **BYD**: China's dominant EV brand. Surpassed Tesla in total EV deliveries in Q3 2024. Expanding aggressively into Europe and Southeast Asia.
- **Volkswagen Group**: Committed €180 billion to EV transition through 2030. Struggling with software delays in its ID. series. Recently announced 35,000 job cuts.
- **Ford**: Announced $12 billion EV investment. Its F-150 Lightning faced production slowdowns due to battery supply chain issues.
- **Rivian**: Amazon-backed EV startup. Delivers electric delivery vans to Amazon but faces profitability challenges.

### Infrastructure & Energy

- **ChargePoint**: Largest EV charging network in North America with 300,000+ charging ports.
- **Electrify America**: Volkswagen-funded fast charging network. Partnered with Walmart to install chargers at 1,200 locations.
- **CATL**: World's largest battery manufacturer. Supplies Tesla, BMW, and Volkswagen. Controls 37% of global battery market.
- **LG Energy Solution**: Korean battery giant. Building new gigafactory in Arizona with $5.5 billion investment.

### Consumer Advocacy Groups

- **Sierra Club**: Pushing for stricter EV mandates and opposing fossil fuel subsidies.
- **American Automobile Association (AAA)**: Reports 42% of US consumers are "very likely" to consider an EV as their next vehicle.

## Market Dynamics

### Adoption Trends
- Global EV sales reached 14 million units in 2023, up 35% year-over-year.
- Norway leads with 82% EV market share. China at 38%. USA at 9%.
- Range anxiety remains the #1 consumer concern, cited by 58% of non-EV owners.

### Price Pressures
- Average EV price in the US fell to $53,000 in 2024, down from $65,000 in 2022.
- BYD's Seagull sells for under $10,000 in China, disrupting global pricing expectations.
- Battery prices dropped below $100/kWh for the first time in 2024.

### Supply Chain Issues
- Lithium mining concentrated in Chile, Australia, and Argentina (the "Lithium Triangle").
- Cobalt sourcing from Democratic Republic of Congo raises ethical concerns.
- US and EU restricting Chinese battery imports, creating supply chain fragmentation.

## Public Sentiment

Social media analysis shows polarized opinion:
- **Pro-EV communities**: Enthusiastic about Tesla Cybertruck, range improvements, and charging speed.
- **Anti-EV sentiment**: Concerns about grid capacity, charging infrastructure gaps, and upfront costs.
- **Rural communities**: Strong resistance to EV mandates due to lack of charging infrastructure and long distances.
- **Auto workers unions**: Mixed. UAW supports EV transition but opposes job cuts. Strike in 2023 secured higher wages.

## Key Controversies

1. **Used EV battery disposal**: No scalable recycling solution exists yet.
2. **Grid readiness**: Widespread EV adoption could increase US electricity demand by 25% by 2035.
3. **Chinese EV tariffs**: US imposed 100% tariff on Chinese EVs in May 2024. EU imposed 38% tariff.
4. **Dealer resistance**: Traditional car dealerships in 30 US states lobbied against direct-to-consumer EV sales.
